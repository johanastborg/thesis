\begin{abstract}
John von Neumann and Francis Murray worked together primarily at the Institute for
Advanced Study (IAS) in Princeton, New Jersey, in the 1930s and 1940s. This is where
they collaborated on groundbreaking work in functional analysis and operator algebras,
which became the foundation for von Neumann algebras (also called operator algebras).
Their contributions were pivotal in the development of mathematical physics, quantum
mechanics, and other areas of mathematics.
\end{abstract}
